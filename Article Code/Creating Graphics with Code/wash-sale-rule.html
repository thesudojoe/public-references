üîç What is a Wash Sale?

A wash sale occurs when you sell a security at a loss and then purchase the same or substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale. This creates a 61-day window where the wash sale rule applies.

üìù How It Works - Example

üíº Real-World Scenario

Step 1: You buy 100 shares of XYZ stock for $10 per share ($1,000 total)

Step 2: Stock drops, you sell for $8 per share ($800 total) = $200 loss

Step 3: Three weeks later, you repurchase 100 shares for $6 per share ($600 total)

Result: üö´ The $200 loss is disallowed for current tax year

üîÑ What Happens to Your Loss?

The disallowed loss isn't lost forever! It gets added to the cost basis of your new shares:

  • New cost basis: $600 (purchase price) + $200 (disallowed loss) = $800
  • If you later sell for $1,000, your taxable gain is only $200 instead of $400
  • The holding period from your original shares carries over

üö´ What You Can't Do

You cannot deduct the loss on your current tax return if you trigger the wash sale rule.

‚úÖ What You Can Do

The loss is added to the cost basis of the replacement security, potentially reducing future taxes.

üéØ What Triggers a Wash Sale?

üîÑ Substantially Identical Securities

The rule applies when you purchase securities that are substantially identical to what you sold, including:

üìà Same Stock

Exact same company shares

üìä Options & Contracts

Options to buy the same security

üè¶ IRA Purchases

Buying in retirement accounts

üë• Spouse Purchases

Your spouse buying the same security

üí° Important: The rule only applies to sales at a loss. Profitable sales are not affected by wash sale rules.

üöÄ Avoiding Wash Sales

‚è∞ Wait It Out

The simplest strategy is to wait more than 30 days before repurchasing the same security.

üîÑ Alternative Investments

Consider purchasing similar but not substantially identical securities, such as:

  • Different companies in the same sector
  • ETFs tracking similar indices
  • Mutual funds with different holdings
‚ö†Ô∏è Day Traders Beware: Pattern day traders who execute frequent trades may encounter wash sales regularly and should consult tax professionals for guidance.

üìã Reporting Wash Sales

üìÑ Tax Forms

Wash sales must be reported on Form 8949 when filing your taxes. Most brokers will identify wash sales on your tax documents.

üíº Professional Tip: Keep detailed records of all transactions and consider consulting a tax professional, especially if you're an active trader.
